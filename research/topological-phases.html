Topological Phases and Phase Transitions

Overview

My research in topological phases focuses on understanding the emergence and characterization of topological states of matter. These phases are characterized by global properties that are robust against local perturbations, making them particularly interesting for both fundamental physics and potential applications in quantum computing.

Methodology

To investigate these systems, I employ a combination of:

  • Exact Diagonalization: For small system sizes to obtain exact results
  • DMRG: To access larger system sizes while maintaining accuracy
  • Time Evolution: Using MPS-based methods to study dynamics
