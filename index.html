Welcome to My Research Page

Welcome to my research homepage. My research spans a diverse array of topics, including quantum phases in strongly correlated systems, topological phases and phase transitions, topological Thouless charge pumping, nonequilibrium physics in closed quantum systems, many-body localization in the presence of disorder, kinetically constrained systems, and electron-phonon coupled systems. Through a combination of theoretical modeling and advanced numerical techniques, I have studied several systems to make a contributions to the field.

I use several numerical techniques to analyze these systems. While small systems are dealt with exact diagonalization (ED) methods, I use the Matrix Product State (MPS) based Density Matrix Renormalization Group (DMRG) method and time-dependent versions of MPS methods for larger systems. I also have implemented quantum typically based methods to study systems in finite temperature. Regarding electron-phonon coupled systems, I use the multi-trajectory Ehrenfest (MTE) method, which is a semi-classical approach. For one of our ongoing projects, we have developed a method where we integrate MTE into the time-evolving block decimation (TEBD) method.
